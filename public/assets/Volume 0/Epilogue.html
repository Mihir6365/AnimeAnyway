Epilogue

Misjudging the Future

Intro

“Today is March 11. The recorder is Suzukake Tanji.”

Putting his phone’s camera in recording mode, Suzukake placed it on the desk.

Then, he turned the lens towards himself.

“For a long time, I’ve been in charge of education in this White Room.”

On this day, Suzukake decided to quietly record his research impressions on his phone.

“However, from today, White Room will be frozen for a while. I don’t know anything about politics, but it looks like a politician called Naoe started working towards stopping Ayanokoji-sensei’s return. He’s really making trouble for us. But, I decided to think with the future in mind. A long vacation after all this, that’s not so bad either.”

As if to take a breather, Suzukake turned off the computer’s monitor.

“Humans really are interesting. As with all children, there are times they can learn things that they haven’t been taught. Just like how they can breathe without being taught how to, the children kept in a closed environment, while only slightly, are seeking communication. After noticing this with the education from the first to the fourth period students, after the 5th period students, I decided to introduce a curriculum that encourages communication. Of course, this also caused some inefficiency. The rate of increase of their abilities decreased with the birth of emotions. Despite that, the 5th period students who have a more difficult curriculum are more capable than even the 3rd period students.”

Punishment, and emotions should remain as extras.

Suzukake still hasn’t changed his way of thinking on that.

"Regarding the curriculum of 10 levels, the 4th level of difficulty is applied to the 5th period students, and the 5th level of difficulty is applied to the 6th period students. This is probably the limit. With the 6th level of education we applied to the 7th period students, all of them have already dropped out. We have modified the curriculum since the 8th period and are continuing to use the 4th and 5th levels in combination, mixing them well. In time, these children will become ideal adults. They will be able to integrate into the world as one of its common people."

Then, Suzukake was silent for a moment.

"I guess this kind of thing is easy to figure out when you look at the files. The reason I wanted to leave a recording today is to remember the passion that kept us running. Many children have already learned in, and dropped out of White Room, but the presence of that child... Kiyotaka Ayanokoji, is still significant. His extraordinary ability to learn, adapt, and apply himself. His talent continues to amaze me every day, and his valuation never ceases to grow. The researchers in White Room believe that they can train him in the same way as those children, but in my opinion, that child is the exception. He is even more unique in this distorted environment. A true mutation."

The self-made beta curriculum. A product of education that boasts the highest level of difficulty.

“No… I don’t know if I should say product. Rather, we can’t replicate him. However, even that Kiyotaka wasn’t flawless from the beginning. His studies,karate or boxing, his achievements were normal and did not stand out at first. The greatness came after that. He is able to quickly absorb everything into his abilities. After he learned the basics, he also learned the technique and the extraordinary ability to adapt and deal with anything he touches.”

Even if I close my eyes, Kiyotaka’s figure which is burned into the back of my eyelids does not disappear.

“The remaining children in the 8th year have decreased to 5. Considering that there were 74 at the start, the drop out rate is over 93%. The average dropout rate from the 1st period to the 3rd period is 27%, and 30% after the 5th period. I can surmise that the beta curriculum was reckless, after all. At this time, I was afraid that everyone in the 9th year would drop out. No… Rather, I wished that they would. If by any chance, a child who was able to succeed through a curriculum that humans shouldn’t be capable of was to remain… that is already a monster that I cannot consider as a human being. It shouldn’t exist. As if supporting this theory, heading towards spring, only one child was left in the end. However, the problem is what happens after. That person shows no signs of dropping out even after 10, 11, or 12 years have passed. Let alone drop out, he became able to surpass us researchers and instructors. Adults with superficial knowledge left White Room scratching their heads after only a few days. White Room’s objective in the first place is to continue the education until adulthood. However, 6 more years… It’s impossible. In the near future, that child will surpass our knowledge. This isn’t just a hunch, I’m convinced. At the same time, I can no longer tell. Whether this presence was born because of my curriculum, or whether he is a genetic mutation. How was he able to survive without dropping out? I can’t prove the reason for that. I’m almost going crazy.”

Then - How should we perceive the existence of White Room and Kiyoata in the future?

The one who will decide in the end is Ayanokoji Atsuomi, this facility’s representative, but the opinions between the researchers are largely divided.

“We still haven’t proven if it is possible to create artificial geniuses or not, but I can say that we have proven that White Room can create superior human beings. However, every child must have a cap that will be their upper limit.”

Suzukake looked at the empty cup that had green tea in it a few minutes ago, opened a new bottle of mineral water, and took only the cup and the bottle cap.

“This represents the talent of an educator. This small cap is in other words the limit of a normal educator’s talent. It’ll be easy to understand if you consider this large cup compared to this cap is the talent of the educators in White Room. The children receiving their education increase their own limits in accordance with the educators’ talent. Considering the cap’s size as the limit for an average human, if they receive an education here their talent can reach the size of this cup.”

He poured the new mineral water into the cup.

“Once you reach the upper limit, there is fundamentally no more room for growth. The water spills out, and you become unable to absorb anything new. …No, maybe that isn’t the correct way to represent it. As you absorb new knowledge, your old talents are slowly lost. Without you even noticing it.”

Looking at the water spilling out onto the desk, Suzukake sighed.

“There are a variety of problems ahead. First, there are only a limited number of people with the talent that is the size of this cup. Second, that even if they have the talent, they do not necessarily have the ability to teach others. Thirdly, that it is not always possible to have this amount of talent. The upper limit is the size of this cup, but some individuals are often one or two sizes smaller than that. Of course, there are also cases of children who develop to be one or two times larger than this upper limit, but the ratio of these children is smaller than the former. The most important part is that geniuses in this world are not limited to the size of this cup. They have talent beyond the size of this mineral bottle, or even more. There is no one who has that much talent, and also the skill to educate others. And even if there was, the children would probably never grow up to be bigger than the cup.”

This reality is also clear from the research date up until now.

“A warm education provided to the children, or the other way around, a strict education. No matter which way you go, I think there is a limit to the aptitude a child has.”

The goal of the White Room project is to create geniuses from ordinary people, and raise up human resources that can fight the world.

“It is possible to intentionally create the top 10% of the category of humans. In that sense, White Room is established as an organization that can come up with sure results. However, we may not be able to create the 0.01% that would be able to fight against the world.”

A feeling of defeat as a researcher, indeed.

Suzukake was keenly aware of this through Ayanokoji Kiyotaka.

“That child has no upper limit to his talent right now. He will absorb as much as we teach him. It could be said that he was born a genius, or that this is a result of his education in White Room, but I think that both of these are correct and incorrect. If Kiyotaka had not been educated in White Room, he would probably have been only a moderately talented person. If Kiyotaka’s education continues like this in White Room, he will be an asset that will greatly increase the talent ceiling of the next generation of children. If Kiyotaka were to take my place and nurture these children, I think the children would grow up to be as big as the plastic bottles, instead of cups. …I would love to see the process of that happening.”

An angel and a devil are arguing in my mind.

I wonder how much he would accomplish if he were sent out into the world to lead Japan, instead of staying as an educator in the small White Room.

Which choice would be better for Japan, and for the future?

It is not for me to make the final judgement, but what choice will Ayanokoji-sensei make?

“I’m going to see it all through, and regardless of his choice, I will keep my connection to White Room for my entire life.”

I’ve never had so much fun in my life, and I am filled with more satisfaction than when I was forced to flee from Japan to overseas.

“But - no matter how extraordinary he is, if you ask me whether Ayanokoji Kiyotaka is a true genius or not, I still have my doubts. For his emotional side, he is greatly inferior to even average people. We don’t know how the effects of that will be. He is imperfect.”

While continuing to talk, Suzukake reached out to his phone and stopped the recording.

“That child I’ve produced… I wonder if he will grasp happiness before he dies…”

He felt greatly uncomfortable recording an utterance so unbecoming of a researcher.

Part 1

It was a day when the season just coincided with the blooming cherry blossoms.

I left Saitama, and returned to Tokyo for the first time in several months.

Instead of my home in Meguro where I had settled a few years ago, I drove to my office, which I had not visited for some time.

“I wonder when’s the last time I was here…”

I looked up from my car’s window at the building which could break down any time, and gave the instruction.

After I pulled over to the shoulder of the road, one man came out.

I have been away from the world of politics for a long time, but the time for my return is near.

Naoe, who has been hiding in Kijima’s shadow as a fixer, is now also 80 years old, and even seemed to be seriously ill at some point.

Publicly, he has returned to politics after being cured of his illness, but in reality his life is in a precarious state.

The proof is the acts of sabotage towards White Room and its shadow supporters by the Naoe side. They decided to get rid of me before their own lives would end.

It was indeed a hard blow that I was forced to temporarily freeze White Room, but I’ve come to think that I received just the right amount of time to prepare for my counterattack.

“Naoe got pretty old too, huh.”

My war in the world of politics will start again very soon.

This is the omen and premonition of that.

As if congratulating me, Kamogawa appeared before me, looking just like he did when we spoke in the ryoutei that day.

“It has been a long time, Ayanokoji-sensei. I did not think that you would take the trouble to come here.”

“Might as well. Don’t worry about it. Are you ready?”

We have been conversing on the phone, but I’ve been having fewer chances to meet with him face to face than even with Sakayanagi.

Because a clumsy movement would get caught in Naoe’s web of observation.

“Thanks to you, I have been handling things somehow well. There are no changes with you either, right, sensei?”

“You’re the one who should be called “sensei”, seeing as you’re repeatedly participating in elections.”

As I spoke so half-jokingly, Kamogawa replied with an extremely serious expression.

“Indeed, you are not a politician right now. However, you’ve taken in a lot of investors, and are in charge of White Room, which is well-known only to those in the know.

Those rumours will never die off.”

Indeed, I continued surviving even in this difficult environment.

Although I was chased out of politics, I’ve brought in many wealthy people with White Room as the starting point, and have improved my own finances to a point even beyond my own imagination.

My title as a politician no longer exists, but more people are calling me “sensei” now than ever before.

“I heard that your son has been very successful in White Room.”

“Is that sarcasm? Because we had to temporarily close it down after overdoing it.”

Kamogawa bitterly laughed, but he had the same look in his eyes as he always did.

No, it rather looks like he is continuing to grow and develop.

“I think you already know, but… Naoe-sensei is pulling the strings from the back. I do not think he will make White Room known to the world, lest he burn himself too, but it looks like he is beginning to use any method possible to destroy White Room.”

“If this wasn’t that man’s idea, I think it would have already been destroyed, so. I can see that the other side is having a hard time getting over this point too. What’s his next move?”

“Things are unclear for now. I am somehow within reach of Naoe-sensei’s faction, but because I used to work by your side, they do not completely trust me.”

Even trying to force my way in, I suppose it is difficult to slip past Naoe’s protective web.

It is more important to rather keep hiding on the inside.

“Just - it looks like his physical condition is getting much worse recently.”

Trying to hide his voice, Kamogawa whispered to me.

“I’d like to bury that man with my own hands, but I suppose him dying of illness is the best possible outcome.”

That’s how hard it is to find a gap in Naoe’s defense.

Considering his age, it should be any time now.

“The day of your return is finally getting closer, Ayanokoji-sensei.”

“Yeah. Most importantly, even if that man is gone, that won’t make it easier to stand up on the top of the world of politics. No, I think it will be even more difficult than before.”

“You are right. I think Naoe-sensei was a leading figure even in the world of politics. However, I think that President Kijima will control him and rise even higher.”

If things keep going this smoothly, the day when the record for the longest tenure will be broken is near.

Furthermore, he is still only 60 years old. If I handle things badly, Kijima’s time will continue for 10, or 20 more years.

I, who used to be the young choice, am piling on more and more years.

“This is my last chance to shoot my shot.”

“That is precisely why you want to have the perfect timing to shoot him down, right?”

White Room’s temporary freeze.

I don’t know if it will be half a year, or 5 years, but my biggest worry is this becoming public.

However, my salvation is that the Naoe side also thinks the same way.

There is no doubt that they are somehow trying to bury it from darkness into another darkness.

The car I had arranged arrived, and Tabuchi opened the rear passenger door.

Kamogawa also slowly entered the passenger seat.

“Tabuchi. Are the arrangements done?”

“As planned, the children will be supervised and handled by the temporarily purchased children’s home.”

“Yeah.”

“Your son - are you really sure?”

“I’m not going to treat him specially just because he’s my own son. However, as White Room’s greatest creation, I can’t say that he doesn’t have the “qualification”. So much that even I’m hesitating. That in itself is significant.”

While heading for our destination, I decided to wait for Kiyotaka, who was being taken out of the clinic.

“Still, a counseling clinic… What happened with Kiyotaka-kun?”

“Well, the reason we stopped by here is that there is someone who wants to meet Kiyotaka no matter what. He’s a big investor, so I have no choice.”

“Wants to meet him?”

“It’s shallow. It’s meant to close the wound, but he doesn’t know that it will have the reverse effect.”

Ishida left the clinic and joined us.

“Kamogawa. When was it that you last saw Kiyotaka?”

“Let’s see, I think it has been 5, no, 6 years since I have seen your son. I am looking forward to seeing how well he has grown up.”

“...Looking forward?”

After entering the car, Ishida glared at Kamogawa with a stern expression.

“Huh? Umm, did I say something strange?”

“That thing is a monster. He’s not something you should look at with such soft feelings.”

“Monster..? He is sensei’s son, you know, you should be careful how you-”

“I don’t care. Ishida is also someone who’s been watching Kiyotaka grow from the start.”

He can say whatever he wants about him.

He is more qualified to do so than someone like me, with nothing but a simple blood connection.

The degree of his perfection as a human being is unimaginable, considering he would be a 3rd year in middle school now.

However, there are many things he lacks in exchange for that.

That must be one of the reasons Ishida calls him a monster.

Kamogawa wrinkled his eyebrows at Ishida’s brutally honest words, and stared out the window.

Part 2

The years I’ve spent in White Room are now over 14 years, and I was at the age where I would finish 2nd year in middle school, as the world would call it.

The real world of the outside, which differs from the virtual.

However, I don’t feel as out of place as I thought I would, and am taking it all in.

It’s unclear whether this is because of a curriculum, or the result of something different.

While waiting in a vacant room after Researcher Ishida’s instructions, one man raised his voice.

“Kept you waiting, Ayanokoji Kiyotaka-kun. Thank you for coming here today.”

“You are?”

A new face, around 40 years old.

From his peaceful expression, I could tell that he isn’t related to White Room.

What caught my eyes more than that, was that he had a vase with flowers growing inside.

I haven’t seen this thing before either. Not except in my knowledge or ./assets/Volume 0/images.

“There was a kid I really wanted you to meet. I begged Ayanokoji-sensei to allow me to do so.”

“I cannot comprehend what you are saying.”

“That kid’s heart is really weak, you see, and she can’t go out as much as she wants. She can only keep her composure in her house, and in this clinic. That’s why I had you come here.”

“Are those… Sakura flowers?”

“They were decorating this room, so I had them watered a little. She likes these. She should be back from her medical examination soon.”

Saying so, he put the vase on a table by the window.

“Kiyotaka….!”

While waiting after the lack of explanation, the room’s door was opened after a short while, and my name was yelled out.

She must be at around the same age as me.

The girl is looking over here with wide open eyes.

“I missed you… I missed you so much!”

“You are..?”

“Yuki, I’m Yuki!”

Yuki. I have a memory of that name.

She’s a White Room student who dropped out a long while ago.

It’s only natural that I still have some memories I could not intentionally delete from my mind.

“Why are you here?”

Even if she did not die in the real sense, she is someone who lost everything after dropping out.

A meeting with the dead.

I sense some uncomfort in this, but I wonder what the real objective of this meeting is.

“Since she left Wh-... the same place as you, my daughter Yuki has been getting weaker and weaker. She’s been in low spirits. She couldn’t leave the house, and just kept worrying about you.”

The man who said so while watching from a distance seems to be Yuki’s father.

This is a little different than the smile she showed me when we were little, but I can still sense the fullness of her emotions.

“It really has been a long time, huh? Kiyotaka, have you… have you been in that place ever since?”

She looks frightened to remember the past.

From looking at her father and Yuki, it seems that they do not like saying White Room’s name out loud.

“All 14 years. I went outside for the first time today.”

“You really are amazing, Kiyotaka… What about the other kids? Did everyone else leave?”

“Who knows? They’re all gone already. It’s been years since I became the last one. I don’t know.”

Including this child before me, I never cared about anyone who dropped out.

“Alone… all that time, in that place…? I… I… that place…!”

As the fear she was pushing down started to come out, Yuki’s body started trembling.

“Yuki, please stop remembering that.”

She’s losing her composure after her memories were triggered.

Are all the people who dropped out this miserable?

I did understand one thing, which is that she is the child of someone significant in the financial world.

I can tell that she is being treated politely after dropping out.

However, if she is going to counseling, that means the wound in her heart has not healed.

And a method to try and heal her, was to let her meet someone else from the 4th period… Me.

I have a grasp on the situation, so I have nothing to do in this place any more.

“I’m leaving now.”

“W-wait! But we just met! I, I want to talk a lot more!”

“I don’t have anything to say to you.”

In the first place, if we can’t talk about White Room, then we can’t build up a conversation.

“Please wait, Ayanokoji-kun. Can’t I ask you to chat with her a little? You can talk about anything you want. Even silly, insignificant subjects.”

“What would those be? You understand that this is my first time in the outside world, right?”

“That’s…”

“Of course, if you want me to lie and make things up for the sake of talking, then that’s fine with me. I have no problems with being forced to converse within the limits of my knowledge. That isn’t what you want, though, is it?”

“I, I’m fine. I’m fine with talking about W-w-white Room too, so!”

Even while breathing hard, Yuki grabbed my sleeve, not letting me go.

“You should stop. You don’t have the capacity to talk to me.”

“That’s, that’s not true…! I always wanted to meet with you, Kiyotaka…”

“You should have stopped it there. Meeting me like this, it’s only going to create a gap in your memories and ideals. If you want to get better, you should continue your treatment here.”

It’s enough now, right? If I’m gonna waste my time here, I’d rather look at the scenery outside.

Even if only a little, I am still curious about the outside world.

“Please. Please, just a while-”

Yuki’s father reached out with his arms to block the exit.

“Is that an order?”

“No… It’s…”

“It isn’t, right? White Room’s representative did not give me any concrete instructions.”

“Yeah. Ayanokoji-sensei only promised to let Yuki meet with you. This is my personal request.”

“I will refuse, then.”

“Wha-!?”

“Because I have decided that this is the best choice for her sake.”

“Are you saying that you don’t care about her because she dropped out!?”

That’s exactly right. Children who dropped out don’t matter.

But, it was this man’s fault to call me for the sake of counseling.

“Excuse me.”

“No! Don’t go, Kiyotaka!”

“Nothing’s changed since back when you dropped out, huh?”

“...!”

“You should be grateful that you were blessed with parents, and continue your treatment here. If you expect anything from me, you’ll only be disappointed.”

“No! I, I want to talk with you! Like we could back then!”

Yuki’s extremely infantile way of speaking and reactions have not changed in the least from back then.

“Please wait! Please!”

“Please move.”

“It’s… It’s not just me. Neither my wife nor her sister can reach out to Yuki. They can’t. Yet, she can talk with you. That has to be a big help for her..!”

“Goodbye. We shouldn’t see each other again. I will take my leave now.”

“Nooo! No! Kiyotaka! Noooo!”

The voice of the girl crying and screaming, and the man who can’t hold himself back from cursing at me.

None of it entered my ears. I’m not interested.

Leaving the hospital, I returned to the car that was waiting for me.

The man who peeked out from the passenger seat’s window waved at me.

“Hey, Kiyotaka-kun. We haven’t met, right? My name is Kamogawa-”

I’ve seen this face.

With this in my mind, I sat down on an empty seat in the back.

“...Nevermind, haha. Forget about it.”

He bitterly laughed, scratched the back of his head and looked ahead.

“Please drive.”

“Understood.”

Inside the silent car, I looked out to the outside world through a window.

“Seeing it for the first time, what do you think about the outside world?”

“Nothing.”

At least, it’s not moving anything inside me that I can call emotion.

“Nothing, huh?”

I’m sure that’s what this man, who is my father, is thinking.

That I am looking out the window, but that there are no emotions inside me.

That this is no different for me than looking at the virtual world.

That is a big mistake.

It’s just that it’s easier to make him think that I have it all under my control.

At the very least, that is the most beneficial thing for me to do right now.

There is no need to let this man realize that I am already sharpening my fangs.

“You will continue the White Room curriculum by my side for a while. I will send you back to the facility when White Room reopens.”

“I know.”

Even if my environment changes, there will be no obstacles as long as I have the skills I acquired in White Room.

Part 3

“It’s pretty annoying, isn’t it?”

After taking Kiyotaka to the mansion, I left Kamogawa behind.

“What is?”

“That there is only one ultimate perfect result. If we follow a sound strategy, we should let him devote his life to nurturing people who can surpass Suzukake in the white room. If we do this, there is a possibility that more than one person will emerge who will eventually come close to Kiyotaka.”

“That was the plan from the start, right? Isn’t that good enough?”

“My return to the world of politics is starting to become a reality. That’s what’s making me hesitate.”

“No way…”

“Annoyed by the fact that there is an age limit in the world of politics, this is the first time I’ve thought of such a thing.”

“You want to make him, Kiyotaka-kun, into a politician..?”

“The strategy of making him an educator in White Room and connecting to the next generation is the true nature of White Room. It is an unavoidable project for the sake of allowing Japan to take the lead in the world in the next 50 or 100 years. That hasn’t changed for me.”

However…

“Yet, in order for me to reach the summit of the world of politics, I need a strong ally. The earliest age that Kiyotaka can get the right to be elected and become a parliament member is 25. I’ll be 61 then. I’ll be right on the edge.”

“But, as a politician - he will also need some time to mature and get into the swing of things, right?”

Of course, Kiyotaka will not be able to do anything immediately after becoming a parliament member.

Theoretically however, he can be appointed as prime minister at 25 years old, he will have that right.

He has the potential to be far more useful than the usual parliament members.

“What do you plan on doing..?”

“I don’t have an answer. If I or Kiyotaka take the lead in the world of politics, we can change this Japan in a big way. However, that would mean a delay in education in White Room. That’s why I’m so troubled.”

The most annoying thing is that he has the title of being my children.

When you enter the world of politics, the public thinks that you just want your second generation to take over.

Big disadvantage.

But, I have an idea.

An idea that might put this disadvantage to use.

He lacks feelings of joy, anger, sorrow and pleasure, and I am very uneasy about his emotional side too.

There is a need to improve these.

“If it’s Kiyotaka-kun, I’m sure he’ll work very obediently, so I would expect a lot of things from him.”

Obedience? Even I don’t know to what extent Kiyotaka himself can be controlled.

His brain has already far surpassed mine.

Even if his emotions are thin, I’m sure he’s already thinking far ahead into the future.

On the other hand, I am fortunate that he is naive on some things about the world.

He hasn’t reached the point for me to be cautious of yet.

For now, I should be prepared to change my plans.

I have a strong and unwavering will to take over this country.

“I’ll have you hang out with me for a little longer today, Kamogawa.”

No matter what I do, first of all, I need to take measures relating to the formation of his personality.

“I do not mind, but… What are we going to do?”

A hand appeared to lightly knock on the car’s window, and Tsukishiro naturally entered the driver’s seat.

Not only does this man have influence in the ruling and opposition parties, but he also has connections in the financial world.

I can’t trust him too much as he always stands on the winning side, but his skills are still certain, even at this age.

“You look well, Ayanokoji-san. It seems like you’ve been getting along quite well with the people from the Peace Party.”

“That doesn’t matter. What happened with the thing I asked you for?”

“The arrangements are complete. There will be no evidence left, so there shouldn’t be any problems.”

“Alright. One more thing, there’s something else I want you to ask for what’s ahead of us.”

I told the plan to Tsukishiro and Kamogawa.

Unlike Kamogawa who continued to be surprised as he listened, Tsukishiro took it all in with a calm smile.

“That is very interesting. I want to say that I would welcome the work, but I have gotten older too. Things may not go as they would if I was younger.”

Despite his modesty, this man would not take on something he cannot do.

“You’re the right man. I want to see how far he can go.”

“If you will leave it to me, then that is fine with me. I understand. I shall cooperate when you put this plan to practice. We will need to prepare some of the missing pieces for later.”

I instructed him to drive, and Tsukishiro started the engine.

I told Tsukishiro, whom I did not trust, only about the parts relating to Kiyotaka's future.

But that's not all I'm after.

I need to grasp the weaknesses of Kijima and the Advanced Nurturing High School, an enemy that I will need to crush in the future.

One year after this, Ayanokoji Kiyotaka will enroll in the Advanced Nurturing High School.
